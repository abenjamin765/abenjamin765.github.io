Problem

Millions of animals are currently in shelters and foster homes awaiting adoption.

Questions

What types of animals live in shelters?
Cats and Dogs primarily make up companion animal shelters.
How do people find animals to adopt?
  • About 65% of pet owners acquire their pets free or at low cost.
  • The majority of pets are obtained from acquaintances and family members. 15 - 20% of dogs are purchased from breeders, 10 - 20% of cats and dogs are adopted from shelters and rescues, and 2 - 10% are purchased from pet shops.
  • At least 20% of cats are acquired as strays.
  • Over 20% of people who leave dogs in shelters adopted them from a shelter.

What reasons do people surrender animals?
According to the American Humane Association, the most common reasons why people relinquish or give away their dogs is because their place of residence does not allow pets (29%), not enough time, divorce/death and behavior issues (10% each). The most common reasons for cats are that they were not allowed in the residence (21%) and allergies (11%).

User Definition

This product is being designed for use by individuals or families in need of a pet.

Problem Statement

The most common reasons pets find their way into shelters are misinformation and misaligned expectations. Methods of finding a pet both online and offline rely on the pet-seeker's personal taste and knowledge about the animals they're looking for. There isn't currently an effective way to facilitate a two-way match between animal and household.

Goals

It's important to set goals for a solution. This helps to drive the ongoing conversation about what the end-product is setting out to achieve and sets the parameters of what is being designed.

  • Personalize choices
  • Facilitate longer lasting pet relationships
  • Minimize misinformation and confusion
  • Set expectations for pet-seekers
  • Minimize the risk of shelter returns

Solution

Design an experience that will help connect people looking for a new pet with the right companion for them.

Design Principles

  • Menus should be obvious
  • Every decision point should be intentional
  • Visuals should be bright an energetic
  • Physical metaphors should be use to help guide interactions
  • On-screen instruction to the user should be minimal
  • Controls come secondary to content
  • Design needs to be thumb friendly

Features

Selecting an MVP feature set keeps the initial design focused on achieving the set goals and ultimately pushes the product to market faster. This will also set a solid foundation for new features as the product scales.

  • Human and animal profile creation
  • Pet information validation
  • Human-to-animal matching
  • Online pet listing crawler
  • Communication between pet-seeker and shelter
  • Choosing process

Success

Help an adopter find a pet that matches his or her lifestyle, considering factors including breed, gender, age, temperament, and health status.

Goal check

To define success, the product needs to achieve the goals set at the beginning of the process. Measurements and observations before and after the launch of the finished product highlight how successful it is.

Measurements

  • Pet relationship longevity - This can be measured as part of the in-app experience or with a follow-up survey.
  • Animal repeat entries - Animals who reappear in the database as "for adoption" are likely returned to the shelter.
  • Number of adoptions - The animals adopted using the app now have a home.
  • Adoption to Surrender ratio - This ratio highlights the impact of more informed adoption decisions

Observations

  • The animal adoption process
  • How people make choices about which animal to adopt
  • The tools people use to find animals for adoption
  • Attributes that impact adoption choices
