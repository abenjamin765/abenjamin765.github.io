SnapBot, Launch scheduled for 2018

"...but I don't have a smart phone" - 15% of High School Students

Opportunity

Student participation is a key factor in the success of a fund-raiser. Today, Snap Raise campaign managers go to participating schools to direct students to a web form where they can input email addresses for people they thing might want to support the team. Campaign managers report that during many school visits, some students can't participate because they don't have access to a smart phone or have a disability they makes filling out a web form difficult.

Role

  • Visit schools alongside campaign managers to observe the current process
  • Lead a brainstorming session to generate concepts
  • Partner with technical team to investigate potential solutions
  • Develop tenets for conversation design
  • Model and define MVP feature-set
  • Design work-flow based on existing process
  • Translate work-flow into dialog
  • Partner with product team to organize beta release

Challenges

  • Is having the campaign manager on-site important to the experience?
  • Can we offer a solution for students (~3%) that don't have access to a phone at all?
  • What other factors should we should consider that may impact student participation?
  • Can we ensure data privacy for students?

Solution

  • Accept input from SMS to ensure accessibility across all devices and abilities
  • Make the work-flow available across platforms students already use
  • Re-task campaign managers to focus on motivation
  • Lock all data transmitted into the platform
  • Offer asynchronous responses
  • Automatically schedule reminders to registered-not-launched(RNL) students
  • Send progress updates to the group leader
  • Make the experience positive and rewarding
  • Communicate progress and reward success
  • Provide both proactive and reactive support to group leaders
  • Connect group leaders with their campaign manager when necessary

Targeted Impact

  • Decrease frequency of RNL participants
  • Offer a great experience across all devices for people of all abilities
  • Increase measured participation per student
  • Give campaign managers ~120+ hours back into their schedule per month
  • Increase the total amount raised per campaign
  • Bring communication onto the platform 100%

Donation Page, Launch scheduled for 2018

"Help me make fund-raising suck just a little less." - High School Softball Coach

Opportunity

Snap Raise was founded to help connect communities and relieve the stress placed on educators to fund extracurricular activities for students. By removing the barriers between students who are active in school sports and clubs and people in their community passionate about supporting these programs, Snap Raise can maximize a group organizer's attention on leading their program.

Role

  • Analyze existing donation experience
  • Identify opportunities with measurable impact
  • Lead brainstorming session to align on customer needs
  • Find behavioral patterns
  • Model and prioritize feature-set
  • Design updated customer-facing UI
  • Develop high-fidelity prototype
  • Organize and execute user-testing
  • Coordinate with technical team to build and release final product

Challenges

  • What is creating friction in the current experience?
  • What's the best way for a student to reach the right people?
  • Will making it easier to donate result in more donations?
  • How can we motivate a site visitor to make a donation?

Solution

  • Give the student ownership
  • Offer the options people use most
  • Make payments simple
  • Don't require registration
  • Make donating feel rewarding
  • Offer a great experience across all devices
  • Remove distractions

Targeted Impact

  • Help more teams achieve their goals
  • Reduce frequency of abandoned sessions
  • Increase organic traffic
  • Increase average amount donated per fund-raiser
  • Compliance with WACAG 2.0 Standards
  • Increase customer satisfaction

Seller Newsletter, Launched 2017

"Why is it that every email from Amazon feels like a punch in the gut?" - Amazon Seller

Opportunity

A study conducted to identify friction points in the Amazon Seller experience revealed an opportunity to improve communication between Amazon and 3rd party sellers. Sellers gave feedback during interviews saying they felt like the majority of communication from Amazon was negative and didn't offer support when they needed it.

Role

  • Define seller cohorts
  • Conduct additional research
  • Craft narrative format
  • Design newsletter layout
  • Align customer needs with business goals
  • Produce and deliver the newsletter

Challenges

  • What content is useful to sellers?
  • Can we make communication go both ways?
  • How can we align our priorities with sellers needs?
  • Will we get feedback and how do we handle it?
  • How do we source regionally relevant material?

Solution

  • Deliver a newsletter to Amazon sellers across all 12 global markets
  • Make the content helpful and relevant
  • Translate the newsletter to match sellers' language settings in Seller Central
  • Gather feedback from sellers after each episode
  • Customize content and messaging to suit seller cohorts and region
  • Highlight new policies, marketplace updates, and opportunities for sellers to grow their businesses

Impact

  • The Seller Newsletter was successfully launched worldwide across 12 markets and translated to 8 languages in May 2017 and continues to be released monthly to-date
  • Open-rate exceeded expectations at 34% with the first edition and a 0.5% increase month-to-month
  • Satisfaction score averaged 4.2/5 for the first 6 months
  • Seller Trust Index Score increased over 3 months
  • Feedback from the newsletter survey provides valuable insights that help to shape the newsletter and contributes to improvements in the Amazon Marketplace
  • The newsletter project helped to inform and shape the broader communication strategy with sellers
  • Content collected and published in the newsletter will soon be pushed across existing channels as well as new ones like Facebook and Twitter to reach more sellers
  • After the 4th newsletter, internal distribution started to the Amazon Marketplace organization and to executive leadership
