Home Depot Order Fulfillment: Modify OrderA way for associates to seamlessly update and fulfill customer orders

TL;DR

Home Depot associates need to be prepared to help customers at every turn. What if we gave them the power to make adjustments to customer orders on-the-go?

Introduction

Kano Study

Feedback & Insights

  • Price adjustments are frequently requested or needed by customers
  • Store associates need a better, more consistent way to resolve customer conflicts
  • Customers expect damaged items to be marked down
  • Price negotiation with Pro Customers earns customer trust and improves business relationships
  • Post-sale refunds and other work-arounds are inconsistently used for price adjustments

My role

  • Gather insights from store associates, customers, and business stakeholders
  • Analyze data from stores across regional markets
  • Develop and measure OKRs
  • Design experiments to test different solutions
  • Build and test prototypes in stores
  • Support change-management
  • Document and deliver technical specs
  • Develop UX roadmap for feature evolution

Strategy

Goals & Objectives

  • Improve in-store customer satisfaction scores
  • Recover lost business due to unexpected circumstances
  • Improve store associate VOA scores (job satisfaction)
  • Decrease product shrink due to avoidable post-sale refunds
  • Maintain positive average sale margin on adjusted orders

Constraints & Scope

  • Design for customer satisfaction, damaged item, and offer mismatch scenarios
  • Require permission for adjustments over $50
  • Keep Order Up adjustments separate from the store register
  • Limit to U.S. market
  • Consider line-items with discounts applied

Execution

Iteration

Store pilot

Feedback (30 days in pilot)

  • Order Up System Usability Score (SUS) improved from 65 to 80
  • Store CSAT scores improved 6%
  • Cost adjustment was the most requested feature in Order Up
  • Transaction times were within average range when cost adjustments were applied
  • Product shrink decreased 4.3%
